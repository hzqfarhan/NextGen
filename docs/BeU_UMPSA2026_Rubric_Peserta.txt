R U B R I C : P R E L I M I N A R Y R O U N D 
 

 
Each item is scored from 0 to 5. Whole numbers only. 

 
Clarity of Problem & Solution (25%) 

Pillar: Technical | Does the team clearly define a real problem and present a logical, understandable solution? 
 

Score Definition 

0 No clear problem. Solution is missing or does not make sense. 

1 Problem is vague. Solution is hard to follow. 

2 Problem is there but poorly explained. Solution has unclear parts. 

3 Problem is well-defined. Solution mostly makes sense with small gaps. 

4 Problem is sharp and backed by context. Solution is clear and structured. 

5 Exceptional. Problem is framed with real insight. Solution story is tight and convincing. 

 

 

Feasibility & Practicality (20%) 
Pillar: Technical | Is the proposed solution realistic and possible to build? 

 

Score Definition 

0 Not realistic at all. No way to build this. 

1 Mostly unrealistic. Needs a complete rethink. 

2 Partly possible but major challenges are ignored. 

3 Possible with some changes. Team knows the constraints. 

4 Very doable. Clear approach with the right tools chosen. 

5 Ready to build. Everything is thought through and the path is clear.

Pillar: Innovation | Does the solution bring something fresh? Is it more than a copy of what already exists? 
 

Score Definition 

0 Direct copy of something that already exists. 

1 Very little originality. Mostly copied from known solutions. 

2 Some creative bits but the main idea is common. 

3 Clearly original. You can tell this is different from existing stuff.

5 Exceptionally creative. Changes how you think about the problem entirely

Score Definition

T H R E S H O L D  &  T I E - B R E A K E R  
 

Minimum Score to Advance 

• Teams need at least 50 out of 100 in the Preliminary Round to move to the Final. 

• Any team that scores 0 on more than one item is automatically out. 

• The top teams advance as decided by the organiser (usually the top 10). 

Tie-Breaker Rules 

If two or more teams have the same score, we go through these steps one by one until the tie is 

broken: 

1. Highest combined Technical pillar score. 

2. Highest score on Uniqueness & Originality (Preliminary) or Functionality & Demo (Final). 

3. Highest score on Feasibility (Preliminary) or Code Quality (Final). 

4. Panel discussion with majority agreement. Head Judge has the final say if needed. 

Judging Across Tracks 

Each track is judged on its own first. Rankings are done within tracks. If there are grand prizes that go 

across all tracks, the same rubric is used so every team is compared fairly.

C O M P L I A N C E  &  D I S Q U A L I F I C A T I O N  
 

Originality 

• All work must be original and created during the hackathon. 

• You cannot submit old projects, previous hackathon entries, or commercial products. 

• Open-source libraries and tools are fine, but you must list them in your README and pitch 

deck. 

• Teams keep the rights to what they build unless the organiser says otherwise. 

Plagiarism 

• Submissions may be checked with automated tools and manual review. 

• Copying large amounts of code, designs, or content from other teams or outside sources 

without giving credit means instant disqualification. 

• Judges can ask any team to walk through their code at any time. 

AI Tools 

AI is encouraged (see Case Study for full details). The short version: 

• Use any AI tools you want. List them in your README under "AI Tools Used." 

• You must be able to explain your own code during Q&A. If you cannot, judges will lower your 

score. 

• Code that is clearly just AI output with no real understanding behind it will be flagged. 

Team Rules 

• Follow the organiser’s rules on team size (check UMPSA registration guidelines). 

• All members must be registered. No swaps after the deadline without organiser approval. 

• You can only be on one team. 

Instant Disqualification 

Any of the following will get your team removed immediately: 

1. Plagiarism or submitting old work.
